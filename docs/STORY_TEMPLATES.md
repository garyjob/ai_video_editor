# Story templates for short-form cuts (Reels / Shorts)

This document captures **Agroverse-style** narrative spines: reusable **beat checklists** for ~15–60s vertical video. Same rhythm across templates; **nouns and circumstances** change.

**Beat:** a small *turn* the audience feels—one job (hook, specificity, payoff)—not necessarily a full scene.

**How to use (future):** pick a template name + optional tone flags (humor-first, awe-first). Optionally override beats. The editor (human or automated) maps beats to **available footage**—empty slots skip if there is no honest match.

---

## Shared principles

- **You curate the folder:** rushes are already on-theme; the template shapes **coverage**, not topic discovery.
- **Chronological filenames** help; templates assume you can order clips sensibly in time when needed.
- **30 seconds** fits roughly **5–9 beats** at ~3–6s each; longer targets allow more beats.
- **Reels performance:** favor moments that are **funny** (surprise, relatable mishap, banter) or **awe / delight** (beauty, scale, first-time wonder, satisfying milestones)—when faithful to the footage.
- **Dignity:** center **agency and competence**; avoid spectacle of suffering or exoticizing communities.

## Template 1 — **Roast / make** (kitchen transformation)

**Promise:** raw material becomes chocolate (or a clear mid-step); **human payoff** is taste, smell, reaction.

### Default beats (in typical order)

| # | Beat | Role |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | **Context** | Where we are; what we’re doing (quick, not a lecture). |
| 2 | **Heat / action** | Roasting, stirring, machinery—visceral motion. |
| 3 | **Sensory hook** | Pop, smell line, rhythm change—**humor** or **tension** often lives here. |
| 4 | **Hands-on craft** | Crack, winnow, grind, temper—**specific technique**. |
| 5 | **Taste (nib / liquor)** | First sensory verdict—mini **awe** or **honest reaction**. |
| 6 | **Drink / final bite** | Hot chocolate or bar—**emotional landing**, comment, laugh. |

### Typical failure modes

- **One-note B-roll:** many similar shots of the same skillet/stir—reads as “same beat” on repeat.
- **Missing payoff:** great process, no **face + reaction**—weak for Reels sharing.
- **Over-length process:** entire short in middle of pipeline; **sip/comment** never gets a slot.

### Tone toggles

- **Humor-first:** prioritize banter, mishaps, timing around pops or spills.
- **Awe-first:** prioritize first reveal, steam, gloss, “first time” language, beauty of transformation.

## Template 2 — **Field / forest** (origin + craft)

**Promise:** we go to where cacao lives; **specialist knowledge** and **place** are the stars.

### Default beats

| # | Beat | Role |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | **Journey / arrival** | Into the farm or forest—movement, stakes, light comedy possible. |
| 2 | **Adventure or humor** | Mud, hike, gear, language, small chaos—**hook** energy. |
| 3 | **Harvest or care work** | Cutting, carrying, pruning, fertilizing—**stages** real to this site. |
| 4 | **Farmer specificity** | The gesture *these* people use—insider detail (credibility + **awe**). |
| 5 | **Payoff** | Pile of pods, taste of pulp, weight, weather turning—**landing** beat. |

### Typical failure modes

- **Walking treadmill:** endless transit; no **craft** or **face** beat.
- **Generic nature B-roll:** could be any forest; missing **specific practice**.

### Tone toggles

- **Humor-first:** journey friction, outsider learning beats.
- **Awe-first:** canopy light, scale of harvest, generational calm.

## Template 3 — **Festival / community** (cultural arc)

**Promise:** cacao (or food) inside **collective life**—rhythm, faces, symbolism.

### Default beats

| # | Beat | Role |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | **Atmosphere** | Sound, color, density—*this is alive*. |
| 2 | **Hook** | One strong funny beat **or** one breathtaking scale/detail. |
| 3 | **Participation** | You or hosts **doing** with the crowd—not only observing. |
| 4 | **Ritual / craft detail** | The specific ceremonial or culinary move—**cultural specificity**. |
| 5 | **Emotional landing** | Shared meal, night shot, gratitude, exhaustion-laugh—**belonging**. |

### Typical failure modes

- **Wallpaper crowd:** beauty without **participation** or **named humanity**.
- **Exotica drift:** spectacle without **agency**; avoid poverty / “othering” framing.

### Tone toggles

- **Humor-first:** micro-interactions, kids stealing scene, gentle chaos.
- **Awe-first:** scale, night processions, music, collective voice.

---

## Template 4 — **Supply chain / move** (A → B + friction)

**Promise:** cacao **travels**; **something happens** between points—the story is **movement + contingency**.

### Default beats

| # | Beat | Role |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | **What / why / A→B** | Cargo, origin, destination, stakeholder—clear in one breath. |
| 2 | **Journey felt** | Road, water, loading, clock—**motion with stakes**. |
| 3 | **The incident** | Delay, weather, paper, breakdown, kindness, absurd border—**core turn**. |
| 4 | **Response** | People **solving**—competence is engaging. |
| 5 | **Outcome** | Arrived, partial win, honest “to be continued”—**closure type chosen**. |

### Typical failure modes

- **Mystery cargo:** viewer never knows **what** moves or **why it matters**.
- **Hardship porn:** obstacle as spectacle; weak if dignity and system aren’t centered.

### Tone toggles

- **Humor-first:** logistics absurdity, misunderstandings, Plan B comedy.
- **Awe-first:** scale of port, river, mountains of sacks, night loading.

---

## Template 5 — **Meta: coverage + diversity** (editorial overlay)

Not a location arc—a **constraint layer** on any template above when automation or assistants compile from many clips:

- Prefer **≤1–2 segments** from the same **repetitive visual beat** (e.g. same skillet hero unless it’s clearly different function).
- Prefer **spread across chronology** when folder order reflects the day (don’t only use first N files if more rushes exist).
- Reserve slots for **human channel** (face + speech reaction) if any candidate exists.

Use this as a checklist when reviewing machine-generated cuts.
